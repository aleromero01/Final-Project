What is it?


This 31-day challenge was created by five high school students who want to help you help your environment. By doing small actions everyday, you get to make a big change for the planet and all animals, plants, and people that live in it. In today's age of technology, it is more than essential to be conscious about how our actions impact our surroundings, and about how we can reduce this impact through the use of technology. Join the challenge, and make a change!

Tips


  • Try to buy things with less packaging.
  • Fix your leaky toilet/ faucet.
  • Combine your errands.
  • Wrap gifts in fabric and tie in ribbon, both are reusable.
  • Buy your own hive and save the bees.
  • Choose energy efficient appliances.
  • Buy compact fluorescent light bulbs. They last 8 times as long and use a fraction of the energy.
  • Drink organic beer.
  • Coffee grounds and eggshells are ideal for composting.
  • Paint your walls a pale color so you need less artificial light.
  • Ban blinds. Heavy curtains keep in more heat in winter.
31-Day Challenge
1st: Brush your teeth with the water off. You might be wasting 200 gallons of water a day.
2nd: Make a playlist that is 15 minutes long for when you are showering so after the music ends you know to turn the water off.
3rd: Turn the water off while you're applying soap/shampoo/conditioner.
4th: If you are going to drive, try carpooling with others. Carpooling is another easy way to reduce carbon dioxide emissions. If you join just one other person on a 50-mile round-trip, you’ll reduce your monthly emissions by almost 10 percent.
5th: Instead of driving, ride bikes or go on buses. A single person who swaps a 20-mile round-trip commute by car to public transportation can reduce annual carbon dioxide emissions by 4,800 pounds.
6th: Instead of buying plastic water bottles use reusable bottles.
7th: Try drinking tap water instead of bottled. Plastic from water bottles are very polluting and take centuries to decompose in landfills.
8th: Plant a tree.
9th: Start a compost heap. Recycling nutrients back into the ecosystem, which keeps food out of landfills and waterways and enriches the soil.
10th: Clean up the beach.
11th: Use tupperware instead of plastic wrap and sandwich bags.
12th: Grow your own fruits and veggies so they so they don't leave a carbon imprint. The practice has a number of benefits: Perhaps most significantly, it reduces the use of fossil fuels involved in transporting produce all over the world. Those who grow their food without pesticides and herbicides also save the planet from extra air and water pollution.
13th: Join a library instead of buying books.
14th: Recycle your car oil at a recycling depot or petrol station; it contains lead, nickel and cadmium.
15th: Only run full loads of laundry.
16th: Cook for a friend.
17th: Instead of plastic bags buy reusable ones when grocery shopping.
18th: Paper of plastic? PAPER.
19th: When washing clothes use a wash line instead of using the dryer.
20th: Turn off TVs and stereos instead of switching them to standby.
21st: Clean the back of your fridge. Dusty coils can increase energy consumption by 30%.
22nd: Shop online instead of going to the store. One survey found as many as 70 percent of online shoppers say they prefer to buy from their favorite retailer online. Buying online involves less energy use and fewer carbon dioxide emissions.
23rd: Try to work at home if you can. Working from home always saves more energy than working from an office because you don’t have to drive there and back. Employees use twice as much energy at an office than at home.
24th: Don't fill up your plate with food start off with small portions and if you are still hungry get a little more. Instead of filling empty plates, that wasted food usually ends up in landfills and eventually turns into methane.
25th: Combine errands. Taking one big trip to pick up groceries, drugstore items, and dry cleaning instead of making each a separate adventure is one easy way to cut down on gas emissions.
26th: Choose to reuse. Plastic and glass can easily be saved and reused for other purposes, like storing all those bulk goods that you stocked up on (or using an empty pickle jar as a way to display photos).
27th: Make preservation your jam. Here’s one tip that surprised us: It’s possible to freeze certain fruits and veggies for up to a year and a half! The key is to freeze them when they’re at their freshest.
28th: Repurpose junk mail as scrap paper.
29th: Eat your leftovers, don't toss them.
30th: Try line drying your clothes.
31st: Turn off all the lights when you leave a room.

Climate Change

Over the past 50 years, the average global temperature has increased at the fastest rate in recorded history. And experts see the trend is accelerating: All but one of the 16 hottest years in NASA’s 134-year record have occurred since 2000. Scientists say that unless we curb global-warming emissions, average U.S. temperatures could increase by up to 10 degrees Fahrenheit over the next century. Global warming occurs when carbon dioxide and other air pollutants and greenhouse gases collect in the atmosphere and absorb sunlight and solar radiation that have bounced off the earth’s surface. Normally, this radiation would escape into space—but these pollutants, which can last for years to centuries in the atmosphere, trap the heat and cause the planet to get hotter. That's what's known as the greenhouse effect.

  • Melting glaciers, early snowmelt, and severe droughts will cause more dramatic water shortages and increase the risk of wildfires in the American West.
  • Rising sea levels will lead to coastal flooding on the Eastern Seaboard, especially in Florida, and in other areas such as the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Forests, farms, and cities will face troublesome new pests, heat waves, heavy downpours, and increased flooding. All those factors will damage or destroy agriculture and fisheries.
  • Disruption of habitats such as coral reefs and Alpine meadows could drive many plant and animal species to extinction.
  • Allergies, asthma, and infectious disease outbreaks will become more common due to increased growth of pollen-producing ragweed, higher levels of air pollution, and the spread of conditions favorable to pathogens and mosquitoes.

Mission

“Ctrl+S Earth” was created in an effort to do something about the wasteful habits of people around the world. Our goal is to facilitate saving methods by generating fun every day challenges for the user. The Earth is our home, and we should treat it like it. It’s also not as difficult to reduce waste as it’s made to be. If everybody in the world at least followed one of our challenges, it would make a world of a difference. It all starts with you.
